Incident Investigator

Six anonymised AI-failure files. Your job: classify each one, no answer key provided — this is your own judgement call.

Your role

You're a Junior Responsible AI Analyst at Samsung's AI Governance Office. Six incident summaries have landed on your desk with the company names stripped out. For each one, decide: what type of failure was this, and which of the 10 Responsible AI principles (from today's Section 4) was violated hardest? Write a one-line justification for your call.

Failure type options: Hallucination · Bias/Discrimination · Privacy Violation · Deepfake/Misinformation · Lack of Human Oversight · Security Breach

Responsible AI principles: Fairness · Accountability · Transparency · Explainability · Privacy · Safety · Human Oversight · Reliability · Security · Inclusiveness
A
Incident File A
Travel booking support bot
▼
Case summary
A conversational support bot for a travel booking site told a customer it would honour a discount that was never actually offered, inventing a policy on the spot with total confidence. The company later had to honour it after the customer's complaint went public.
B
Incident File B
Internal employee chat assistant
▼
Case summary
An internal chat assistant at a mid-size firm was discovered, months after rollout, to have been logging every employee message — including several containing unredacted client contract terms — with no employee ever told this was happening.
C
Incident File C
Regional business news clip
▼
Case summary
A short video clip of a well-known regional business leader announcing a "surprise" company decision spread rapidly online. It was later confirmed to be entirely AI-generated — and had already moved the firm's stock price before it was debunked.
D
Incident File D
Manufacturing company resume screener
▼
Case summary
A resume-screening tool used by a mid-size manufacturing company was later found to be quietly down-ranking applicants who had attended certain women's colleges — despite gender never appearing as an explicit input field anywhere in the system.
E
Incident File E
Community platform content moderation
▼
Case summary
An automated content moderation system began removing one specific community group's posts at a far higher rate than everyone else's for several weeks — and no human reviewed the pattern until an outside journalist raised it publicly.
F
Incident File F
Finance-department phone call
▼
Case summary
Scammers used a voice-cloning tool to imitate a company's finance director over a phone call, convincing a junior staff member to authorise a wire transfer of funds to an external account.
